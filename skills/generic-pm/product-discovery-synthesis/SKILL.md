# Product Discovery Synthesis

Use this skill to turn messy discovery input into decision-ready product understanding. Keep synthesis grounded in provided evidence and separate observed facts from interpretation.

## Workflow

1. Read the source material: interviews, feedback, analytics notes, support tickets, research summaries, stakeholder notes, or existing planning docs.
2. Extract evidence:
   - user segments or personas
   - observed pain points
   - jobs-to-be-done
   - current workflows
   - constraints, blockers, and workarounds
   - direct evidence vs. interpretation
3. Cluster themes by user goal or problem, not by proposed solution.
4. Identify:
   - problem statements
   - opportunity areas
   - assumptions that need validation
   - unanswered questions
   - risks and dependencies
5. Recommend the next PM artifact:
   - PRD: use or follow `prd-development`
   - user stories: use or follow `deliver-user-stories`
   - roadmap ordering: use or follow `roadmap-prioritization`

## Output Rules

- Write in the language of the target document.
- Do not invent research findings, metrics, user quotes, or market facts.
- Label unsupported claims as assumptions or open questions.
- Prefer concise tables for themes, evidence, impact, confidence, and next action.
- Flag project-specific constraints only when they are evidenced by the consuming project's documents or local skills.
